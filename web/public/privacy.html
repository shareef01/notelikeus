Privacy Policy

Notelikeus · Last updated: July 2026

Notelikeus is an offline-first notes application. This policy describes how the app handles information on your device and when you optionally enable cloud sync.

Summary

  • Notes are stored locally on your device in an encrypted database (Android) or browser storage (web).
  • Optional cloud sync uploads note text to your Firebase account when you sign in with Google and choose to sync.
  • Locked notes are not uploaded to the cloud.
  • The app does not include analytics or advertising SDKs.

Information stored on your device

The app may store note titles, body text, colors, labels, checklists, reminder times, and settings (theme, app lock, auto-sync preferences).

Optional cloud sync

If you sign in with Google and use sync, note content (except locked notes) is stored in Google Firebase Firestore under your Google account. Data is governed by Google’s privacy policy and your Firebase project settings.

Security

  • Android: notes are stored in a SQLCipher-encrypted Room database.
  • Web: notes are stored in local browser storage on your device.
  • Optional biometric lock uses your device’s biometric APIs.
  • Locked notes are not synced to the cloud.

Backup export/import uses the system file picker; the app only accesses files you select.

Backups

JSON backup export/import is manual. You are responsible for securing copied files.

Third parties

Google Firebase is used only when you enable cloud sync. We do not sell your personal data or use analytics or advertising SDKs.

Children

Notelikeus is not directed at children under 13.

Changes

We may update this policy as the app evolves. Material changes will be reflected here and in the in-app privacy text.

Contact

For privacy questions, reach out via the app’s store listing or the project repository.
